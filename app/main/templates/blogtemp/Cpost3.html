Why Should You Exfoliate Your Lips?

Your lips deserve some love, and exfoliation is the perfect way to give it to them. Unlike the rest of your skin, your lips don’t have oil glands to keep them hydrated, so they’re prone to drying out and flaking.
Regularly exfoliating sweeps away dry, dead skin, leaving behind a smoother texture that feels great. But that’s not all—the benefits of lip exfoliation go beyond just softness. It can enhance your natural lip color by revealing fresher skin underneath, making them look brighter and healthier.
Plus, it preps your lips for balms or lipstick, so they glide on effortlessly without catching on rough patches. By keeping up with this simple habit, you’ll prevent buildup and cracking, ensuring your lips stay kissably soft all year round. Who doesn’t want that?

How to Exfoliate Lips?

This one’s a fan favorite, and for good reason—it’s simple and feels like a treat. Grab 1 teaspoon of sugar (brown or white works) and mix it with 1 teaspoon of honey or olive oil. The sugar gently buffs away dead skin, while the honey or oil keeps things moisturized. Rub the mixture onto your lips in small, circular motions for 1-2 minutes. Rinse it off with lukewarm water, pat dry, and voilà—smooth lips! Bonus: it smells yummy too.

What Experts Say About Lip Exfoliation?

Wondering what the pros think? Dermatologists are all about lip exfoliation—when it’s done right. “Gentle exfoliation can remove dead skin and improve lip texture,” says Dr. Jane Smith, a board-certified dermatologist. “But it’s all about balance. You want to clear away buildup without stripping the natural oils that protect your lips.”
