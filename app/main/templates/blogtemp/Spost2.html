Breakouts or burnouts—must we choose?! If you’ve ever skipped sunscreen because you feared waking up with a face full of zits, you’re definitely not alone. “Does sunscreen cause acne?” is one of the most Googled skincare questions out there—and it’s high time we set the record straight.

In this no-nonsense guide, we’re diving into the science, decoding ingredients, and sharing pro tips to help you protect your glow—without triggering those dreaded pimples. Let’s break it down (not out)!

Does Sunscreen Cause Acne for Everyone?

No. A 2024 Skin Research survey of 1,000 sunscreen users found that only 15% reported breakouts, mostly those with pre-existing acne or oily skin. For many, sunscreen is a non-issue—or even a benefit, as some formulas (e.g., with zinc oxide) calm inflammation. Does sunscreen cause acne universally? Far from it—context is everything.

Common Myths About Sunscreen and Acne

Let’s debunk 2025’s trending myths:

  • Myth: All sunscreens cause acne.
    Truth: Only comedogenic or mismatched formulas do—many are acne-safe.
  • Myth: Skipping sunscreen clears acne.
    Truth: UV damage inflames skin, potentially worsening acne long-term.
  • Myth: Chemical sunscreens are worse than physical ones.
    Truth: Both can work; it’s about formulation, not category.

Does Sunscreen Cause Acne Compared to Other Products?

Sunscreen isn’t the only suspect in breakouts:

  • Moisturizers: Often richer, they’re more likely to clog pores if not non-comedogenic.
  • Makeup: Foundations or powders layered over sunscreen can trap oil.
  • Sweat: Exercise without cleansing post-sweat is a bigger acne driver than sunscreen alone.
    • Does sunscreen cause acne more than these? Not inherently—application and product synergy matter most.

The 2025 Take: Why This Matters Now

With UV exposure up 7% globally since 2000 (NOAA) and acne affecting 85% of people at some point (AAD, 2025), does sunscreen cause acne is a timely concern. Modern formulations—think mattifying gels or zinc-infused lotions—cater to acne-prone skin better than ever. In March 2025, as summer nears, balancing UV protection with clear skin is achievable with the right know-how.

Conclusion

So, does sunscreen cause acne? It can, but it doesn’t have to. The risk ties to your skin type, the formula, and how you use it—not sunscreen itself. By choosing non-comedogenic, lightweight options and mastering application, you can shield your skin from UV damage without sparking breakouts. In 2025, sunscreen isn’t just necessary—it’s adaptable. Protect your face, keep it clear, and enjoy the sun worry-free.
